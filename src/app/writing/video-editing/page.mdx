## Video Editing → Developer

In video editing, I see a lot of parallels to what I do with software development: design, implement (create transitions), and deploy.

Obviously, designing was the hardest part; finding footage that fits a song and writing a valid story to tell. The biggest difference is that in video editing, you can hide all your flawed effects implementation, but in coding, you can’t.

This is what drew me into developing. Although I still cherish my experience with editing, I think I can do just as well in developing.

All the edits I’ve seen had so much work put into them, and I can’t help but see that grit in people who develop too.

## Side Note: what I’m thinking of doing.

Sometimes I question: why didn’t I just find a job as a video editor? I do not think AI can replicate anything close to the effects, storytelling, and transitions I’ve designed.
But I’m on my next chapter, and honestly, I still think it’s an amazing hobby to have.

I see myself as a very creative person, so maybe in the future, I’ll start involving video editing in my coding projects, and eventually revolutionize UI.

I also have strong areas in my backend development, knowing how to secure RESTful API calls, dockerize, ship, and more.

In a team, I’d probably be a great fit haha (offer me a job).
